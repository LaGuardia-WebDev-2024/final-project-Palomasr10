PAELLA

  • 2 cups Calasparra rice

  • 4 cups warm chicken broth

  • 1/4 lb shrimp

  • 1/4 lb Manila clams

  • 1/2 lb mussels (beards removed)

  • 1/2 lb chicken breast skin on cut into 1 1/2" by 1 1/2" pieces

  • 1/4 cup tomato paste

  • 1/4 cup peas

  • 1/4 cup finely diced carrots

  • 1/4 cup finely chopped

  • Spanish onion

  • 1/2 red bell pepper thinly sliced and cut to 1" pieces

  • 1 clove garlic finely chopped

  • 2 tbs olive oil

  • 1/2 tsp of roasted saffron strings salt

Heat an 8" paella pan on a medium fire. Add the olive oil and let it heat. Fry the chicken break skin down to get the skin crunchy, then turn to seal the rest of the sides. When ready remove from the pan and set to the side. In the same pan fry the onion till it gets transparent, add the garlic, the carrot and the bell pepper and fry for it for 2 minutes. Push the onion, garlic, carrots and bell pepper to the side of the pan and add the rice. Fry it while stirring it till it turns from becoming transparent to turning white. Push the rice to the side and put the tomato paste in the pan. Let it get hot and then start working the rice into it stirring till it gets coated. Then incorporate the onion, garlic, carrots and bell pepper and mix well. Add the saffron and the chicken broth. Let it boil for 1 minute. Stir to even it all out and bring the fire down to a medium low fire. When the broth has reduced to half, add the clams, the mussels, the chicken, the shrimp and the peas. Continue cooking till the rice is al dente. Serve with lime wedges.

CHICKEN TACOS

  • 2 split chicken breasts with the skin on

  • 10 5" tortillas

  • 1/4 cup canola or avocado oil

  • 10 toothpicks

Place the chicken in a pan, let if boil covered for 30 minutes. Then turn off the fire and let it rest in the pan for 30 minutes. Once rested, take the chicken out the broth and let it cool. Once cooled, using your fingers or two forks shred it fine. Warm up (5at a time) the tortillas in a non-sticky pan on medium fire till flipping once till they become elastic (+/- 1 min per side) and set in the middle of a cloth kitchen towel to keep them warm. Place a handful of chicken on the end of a flat tortilla. Spread it even between center and edges and roll it to form a taco. Insert the toothpick diagonally at the middle top edge of the tortilla as to stop it from rolling open. Set the frying pan to medium high, warm up the oil, and fry the tacos turning them around till the tortillas get golden crisp. Place on a paper kitchen towel to let the excess oil drip off. Serve with guacamole, sour cream and shredded lettuce.
